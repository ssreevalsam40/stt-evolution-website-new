The Technological Journey

Decoding the decades of innovation that brought us here.

1952

Bell Labs "Audrey"

The first true speech recognition system. It recognized spoken digits with high accuracy, but only for a single voice.

1962

IBM Shoebox

A portable machine that could recognize 16 spoken words and digits. A leap towards universal recognition.

1970s

Harpy & DARPA

Funded by the US government, Carnegie Mellon developed Harpy, capable of recognizing 1,000 words.

1997

Dragon NaturallySpeaking

The first continuous speech recognition system for consumers. No more... pausing... between... words.

2011

The Siri Era

Deep Learning meets the mass market. Voice assistants become an integral part of our daily digital lives.

2024+

Whisper & Beyond

Transformer-based models achieving human parity, multilingual robustness, and real-time inference.
